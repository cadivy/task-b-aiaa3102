# Likely Label-Error Audit

## Status

**V1 method specification — model candidates and human adjudication are pending.**

## Audit question

Which public labels are sufficiently inconsistent with independent evidence to justify review, while avoiding the invalid assumption that every model error is a label error?

## Candidate generation

Candidates may be generated by:

- high-confidence out-of-fold prediction disagreement for training rows;
- high-confidence held-out prediction disagreement used only for audit, not relabeling;
- agreement between word and character models against the assigned label;
- nearest-neighbor label disagreement;
- exact-duplicate cluster label conflict;
- repeated model instability or unusually high loss.

The broad candidate list prioritizes recall. It is not the final finding list.

## Independent-signal requirement

The protocol requires at least two independent signals. Accepted combinations include:

| Signal 1 | Signal 2 | Independence judgment |
|---|---|---|
| OOF word-model disagreement | Duplicate-cluster label conflict | Strong |
| Character-model disagreement | Manual policy review | Strong if reviewer is blinded first |
| Neighbor-label majority | Manual policy review | Strong |
| Word-model probability | Word-model predicted label | Not independent |
| Two regularization settings on same TF-IDF | Same model family output | Weak; needs third evidence |

## Review procedure

1. Hide the first reviewer’s conclusion from the second reviewer.
2. Show the raw text, current label and quantitative signals.
3. Ask whether the opposite label is clearly more consistent with the written policy.
4. Record possible missing context.
5. Separate `should_fix`, `ambiguous_policy_case` and false positives.
6. Use the lower confidence when reviewers agree on the decision but disagree on certainty.

## False-positive controls

Reject or downgrade candidates when:

- the model is relying on a known shortcut token;
- the text is too short or context-dependent for a confident semantic judgment;
- the candidate lies close to the decision threshold;
- neighbor evidence comes from a suspected leakage/duplicate artifact only;
- both “independent” models use nearly the same representation and errors;
- the example is better classified as ambiguous than mislabeled.

## Training-label intervention

Never edit the canonical label column. Confirmed training corrections are stored as an overlay:

```text
id,original_label,proposed_label,evidence_refs,review_status
```

The intervention experiment compares the original training set with the overlay condition while leaving held-out labels untouched.

## Limitations

- No model can independently establish ground truth from the same noisy corpus.
- SMS messages often omit sender and relationship context.
- The UCI labels may reflect an annotation policy that is only partially recoverable.
- Relabel-overlay results measure sensitivity to our adjudication, not definitive correction.
